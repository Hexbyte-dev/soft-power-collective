# Competitive Research — The Soft Power Collective

A field scan of peer educators, studios, and communities in the kink-positive, conscious-relationships, and female-led intimacy education space. Research conducted April 2026 to inform positioning, copy, and visual direction.

---

## Primary: Bound Studio

Bound Studio (Lancaster) positions itself as a **professional, relationship-focused shibari and rope studio** — not a dungeon, not underground. Their framing leans explicitly toward "intimacy" and "connection" rather than "kink," which is strategic and worth noting.

**What they do well:**
- Use language like "Discover intimacy," "deepen bonds," "explore together" — relational, not transactional
- Educators are described with professional credentialing language (years teaching, method lineage)
- Small-group format is positioned as a feature (safety, attention) not a limitation
- Photography is clean, modelled, softly lit — aspirational without being explicit
- Couples are an obvious target audience, which broadens appeal beyond the typical single-practitioner market

**What to steal:**
- The move from "kink" to "intimacy" as the primary noun is savvy — it lowers the barrier for new visitors
- Educator-centered marketing ("Learn from [Name]") creates magnetism
- Workshops framed as experiences people have *together*, not skills they acquire alone

## The Field: Female-Led Peers Worth Watching

### Kasia Urbaniak — kasiaurbaniak.com
Former dominatrix, now runs The Academy. Owns the **"women + power"** lane entirely. Her copy uses words like "reclaim," "conditioning," and "the Good Girl" — framing personal liberation as political. Visual identity is stark white with bold serif typography. Serious, academic feel. Pulls in women who'd never call themselves "kinky" but want agency.

### Midori / ForteFemme — fortefemme.com
The original female dominance educator. Runs 9-person intensives. Uses **"Head-Heart-Hands"** as her pedagogical frame. Copy is elegant and practical, never spiritualized. Emphasizes mentorship and lineage. Deliberately small. She's proof that the *scarcity + elite mentorship* positioning works in this space.

### Layla Martin — laylamartin.com
Mainstream tantra/pleasure. **"VITA Method."** Dominates spiritual-sensual Instagram. Copy uses "ignite," "awaken," "sacred," "pleasure," "magnetic." Lush brand photography with lots of flowing fabric, candles, rose petals. Her funnel is enormous — free masterclass → low-ticket offer → high-ticket program. Very polished. Maybe *too* polished for SPC's intended tone, but the funnel architecture is instructive.

### Jessica Fern — jessicafern.com
Author of *Polysecure*. Owns the **attachment theory + non-monogamy** intersection. Copy is warm and therapeutic ("secure," "navigating," "integrating"). Positioned as a psychotherapist first, a polyamory expert second — which gives her enormous credibility. Minimal brand, emphasis on books and talks.

### Barbara Carrellas — barbaracarrellas.com
Urban Tantra author. Pioneer status. Warmer, older, more activist voice. Uses "ecstatic," "expansive," "erotic energy." Her brand is less polished than the newer educators, which actually reads as trustworthy — she's been at this since the 90s.

### Somatica Institute — somaticainstitute.com
**Sex and intimacy coaching certification program.** Copy is earnest, warm, pedagogical. Lots of "somatic," "embodied," "attunement." Teal + warm neutrals palette. They're targeting aspiring practitioners, not end clients, but their language is the bridge between therapy-world and kink-world and a great reference for SPC's voice.

### Lee Harrington — passionandsoul.com
Educator, author, ritual-oriented. Masculine-adjacent but frequently teaches alongside women educators. Gender-expansive framing. Good reference for inclusive language that doesn't default to heteronormative couples.

### Sunny Megatron — sunnymegatron.com
Sex educator, podcast (*American Sex*, *Open Deeply*). Very approachable, conversational voice. Copy reads like a smart friend. She shows what a trustworthy accessible-educator tone sounds like without being condescending.

### The Expansive Group / Curious Fox / similar communities
Community-first brands (not personality-led). Use "members," "gatherings," "space," "collective." Tend to lean into aesthetic minimalism because the *community* is the product, not the founder.

## Patterns Across the Field

### Recurring language

The words that appear repeatedly across successful brands in this space, grouped by theme:

- **Embodiment language:** embodied, somatic, felt sense, come home to yourself, in your body, presence, attunement
- **Power language:** reclaim, agency, autonomy, sovereignty, consent, empowerment, your yes, your no
- **Community language:** collective, gathering, circle, container, space, belonging, held, witnessed
- **Exploration language:** curious, exploration, journey, discover, learning edge, expansive, invitation
- **Safety language:** trauma-aware, inclusive, affirming, consent-centered, held with care, trust, braver space
- **Contrast with avoidance:** the word "kink" is used selectively — often paired with softening nouns like "curiosity," "conscious kink," "intentional play"

### CTA patterns

Nobody in this space says "Buy now." They say:
- "Join a circle"
- "Begin your journey"
- "Explore upcoming offerings"
- "Apply to work together"
- "Find your way in"
- "Get on the list"

**"Apply"** is interesting — it signals selectivity and that the educator is choosing you too, not just taking your money. Midori and Kasia both use it.

### Pricing transparency

Split roughly 50/50. Larger brands (Layla Martin, Somatica) publish clear tiered pricing. Smaller personality-led brands (Midori, Kasia) use "Apply" or "Inquire" — preserving scarcity and allowing price flexibility. **For SPC, "Inquire" is the right call early** — it lets you price based on fit, and it matches the intimate community vibe.

### Visual patterns that work

- **Warm earth palettes** are ascendant (sage, terracotta, cream, rust, soft gold). The old dark-red-leather palette is out.
- **Hand-drawn or organic illustrations** are a strong differentiator — Midori and Barbara Carrellas both use them. They soften the subject matter.
- **Photography:** Real people, natural light, hands + objects (rope, candles, books, tea cups), faces but not always — intimacy without voyeurism.
- **Typography:** Refined serifs for headings (Cormorant, Playfair, Tenor) paired with clean humanist sans (Nunito, Inter, Montserrat).
- **Whitespace is non-negotiable.** Cramped design reads amateur in this space.

## Positioning Opportunities for SPC

### What's crowded

- Tantra / sacred sexuality (Layla Martin owns this)
- Polyamory + attachment theory (Jessica Fern owns this)
- Rope / shibari education (Bound Studio, Lee Harrington, Midori all active)
- Female dominance coaching (Kasia, Midori)

### What's underserved

1. **Warm + rigorous community for kink-curious people who don't identify as "kinky."** Most brands require you to self-identify before they let you in. SPC can be the on-ramp.
2. **Explicit "emotional intelligence meets practical play."** Most educators emphasize one or the other. Your bio beats (C-suite background + personal journey) are a rare combination that lets you credibly hold both.
3. **Community-first, not personality-first.** You're "The Collective," not "Casey Lynn Coaching." That's a structural differentiator — personality-led brands rise and fall with the person, but a collective has room to scale and be plural.
4. **Sliding-scale accessibility without looking cheap.** Most of the market is either $$$$ intensives or bargain-bin. A clear "we believe in making this accessible" stance is distinctive.

## Copy Recommendations

### Headlines / taglines to consider or adapt

1. *"A gentle space for bold exploration."* (your current — this is strong, keep it)
2. *"Curiosity is enough."* (low-barrier entry)
3. *"Where emotional intelligence meets intentional play."*
4. *"Conscious kink. Honest connection. Real community."*
5. *"For people who want to grow, not just learn."*
6. *"The community you wish you'd had when you started asking questions."*
7. *"Kink that makes sense. Connection that lasts."*
8. *"An invitation, not a test."*

### Navigation / button copy

- **"Join a Circle"** (for coaching circles) — feels held and warm
- **"Find Your Pairing"** (for mentorship) — better than "Apply for Mentorship" which is cold
- **"What's Coming Up"** (for events) — friendlier than "Calendar"
- **"Inquire About Pricing"** → upgrade to **"Tell Us About You"** for an intake form (reframes the transaction as a conversation)

### Section transitions / language to weave in

- "Whoever you are, whatever you're exploring, you belong here."
- "We move at the speed of trust."
- "Take what resonates. Leave the rest."
- "This isn't about getting it right — it's about showing up whole."

### Phrases to avoid

- "Lifestyle" as a noun (too insider, alienates newcomers)
- "Kinksters" (infantilizing)
- "Play partner" in marketing copy (too specific/insider)
- Anything that implies "beginners" vs. "experienced" as a hierarchy
- "Dungeon" language

## Aesthetic Recommendations

**Keep:**
- Sage green, terracotta, cream, charcoal palette (you're aligned with the winning trend)
- Serif headings (Cormorant Garamond is ideal)
- Hand-drawn organic illustrations (open hands, crescent moon, rope knot)
- Warm natural-light photography

**Add:**
- Texture — paper grain, subtle linen textures, soft noise. Most competitors have flat brands; texture reads as crafted and intentional.
- Occasional script/handwritten accents for pull quotes or signatures
- Photography of hands and objects (rope, books, candles, herbs) — builds mood without requiring faces
- Seasonal or monthly theming (Layla Martin does this, Somatica does this) — gives content cadence and makes the email list feel alive

**Avoid:**
- Stock photography of "intimate couples" (reads generic)
- Red or black as primary colors
- Anything that looks like a kink club aesthetic (metal, leather, neon, skulls)
- Over-polished influencer-style reels — authenticity wins in this space

## Priority Takeaways for the Website

1. **Your tagline is already strong. Don't let anyone talk you into changing it.**
2. **Lead with "belonging" language on the home page, not "kink" language.** You can introduce kink as you go deeper.
3. **Use "Inquire" not "Buy"** — matches the intimate vibe and maintains pricing flexibility.
4. **The "C-suite + personal journey" angle is your unfair advantage.** Few competitors have both professional rigor AND lived experience. Lean into it.
5. **Community-first framing (The Collective) scales better** than personality-first (Casey Lynn). You already got this right.
6. **Every page should feel like an invitation, not a pitch.**
