# Language Recommendations — The Soft Power Collective

A copy/language review grounded in `competitive-research.md`. The ask: reposition Casey as an **intimacy coach** (her core professional identity) with The Soft Power Collective as the community she's built around that coaching work. No visual or structural changes — copy only. Casey's About Me bio on `about.html` is locked and untouched.

---

## 1. Research summary (key findings that drive copy decisions)

- **"Intimacy" outperforms "kink" as the primary noun at the front door.** Bound Studio and others lower the barrier for new visitors by leading with relational language; "kink" is introduced deeper in the funnel, often softened ("conscious kink," "intentional play"). (Research §Primary: Bound Studio, §Recurring language)
- **The word "kink" is best used selectively and paired with softening nouns.** Avoid it in the hero; it's fine in program copy once the visitor has opted in. (§Recurring language)
- **"Apply" / "Inquire" signals selectivity and a two-way fit.** Midori and Kasia use it; it matches SPC's intimate-community vibe and preserves pricing flexibility. (§CTA patterns, §Pricing transparency)
- **Educator-centered marketing creates magnetism** — "Learn from [Name]" / named-practitioner framing converts. This directly supports the intimacy-coach repositioning. (§Primary: Bound Studio)
- **SPC's unfair advantage is "emotional intelligence meets practical play"** — few competitors credibly hold both. Lean into it. (§Positioning Opportunities #2, §Priority Takeaways #4)
- **Avoid "lifestyle" (as a noun), "kinksters," "play partner," "dungeon," and beginner/experienced hierarchies.** These alienate newcomers and read insider. (§Phrases to avoid)
- **Lead home page with belonging language, not kink language.** (§Priority Takeaways #2)
- **Community-first framing ("The Collective") is already working** — don't undercut it by making the site feel personality-led. Casey can be foregrounded as the coach *within* the collective. (§Priority Takeaways #5)

## 2. Positioning statement

**Primary:** *"Casey Lynn is an intimacy coach guiding people toward honest connection, conscious kink, and a truer relationship with themselves — and The Soft Power Collective is the community she's built around that work."*

**Alternative A (shorter, more declarative):** *"Casey Lynn is an intimacy coach. The Soft Power Collective is the community she grew around her work."*

**Alternative B (more warmth, less titular):** *"Casey Lynn coaches people through the tender work of intimacy — with themselves, with partners, and with the parts of their desire they've never had language for. The Soft Power Collective is how that work becomes shared."*

All three use "intimacy coach" as the professional noun (per §Recurring language and §Priority Takeaways #4), preserve the community framing (§Priority Takeaways #5), and stay in Casey's grounded register (no "unlock," "transform," "ignite").

## 5. Things NOT to change

- **Both taglines:** *"There is empowerment in growth, and growth in empowerment."* and *"A gentle space for bold exploration."* The research explicitly endorses the second one (§Headlines #1: *"your current — this is strong, keep it"*) and the first is her own phrasing.

- **The featured quote text** *("Healing isn't about getting it right — it's about showing up whole, even when we're still in pieces.")* — this is an echo of her own bio and doesn't need a word changed. Only the attribution line gets updated.
- **Peer Coaching Circles description** *("These aren't lectures — they're conversations. You'll be met with curiosity, not judgment.")* — already in voice. Leave it alone.
- **Book descriptions** on `bookclub.html`. They're short, warm, and accurate.
- **The "My Approach" cards on `about.html`** (Emotional Intelligence First, Open to All, Every Dynamic Welcome) — "Every Dynamic Welcome" even lists specific identities which is gently inclusive without being forced. (Per the brief, Trauma-Aware was already removed; don't revisit.)
- **The email signup copy and the form-note privacy line** — they're working.
- **Contact page FAQ #2 (confidentiality)** — the answer is specific and trustworthy; don't soften it.
- **The small italic "Reaching out is an act of courage. We see you."** — this is the best microcopy on the site.

## 6. Open questions

1. **How explicitly should "intimacy coach" appear in the hero?** The primary ask (Section 1D "Meet Casey") now says it plainly, but the hero itself is still brand-forward ("The Soft Power Collective"). The research leans toward *leading with belonging, not with kink* (§Priority Takeaways #2). So I've kept the hero as-is. **Question for Casey:** does she want her name/role in the hero too (e.g., a small sub-line "Intimacy coaching by Casey Lynn"), or is she comfortable with the current sequence where the brand comes first and she's introduced in Section 1D?

2. **Does she want "kink" in the home page hero or Section 1B?** I've kept it in Section 1B (which already has it — *"curious about kink"*) because Section 1B is one scroll down, which is a reasonable "opt-in depth" per the research. But if she wants to go even softer up top, Section 1B could lead with "curious about power dynamics" or "curious about desire" and introduce "kink" in Section 1C cards. Flagging as an available knob.

3. **Naming the 1:1 coaching service.** I've referred to it as "1:1 coaching" and "Working with Casey." If she has her own preferred phrase (e.g., "private sessions," "one-on-one work," "individual coaching"), that should be standardized across the new Contact inquiry option, the programs pricing block, and footer link. The research doesn't dictate here.

4. **Whether to add a proper FAQ about what coaching actually is.** There's currently no FAQ explaining what happens in a 1:1 session with Casey, what it costs roughly, or how long it lasts. The research doesn't mandate one, but it's a reasonable inclusion for the coach repositioning. I've stopped short of drafting it because I'd be inventing details about her actual practice (session length, structure, whether she does packages vs. single sessions).

5. **"The Lifestyle" still appears in one Peer Circles theme label** (*"Open Relationships vs. The Lifestyle"*). I've proposed a rewrite, but if this is the *actual* historical label of a recurring session Casey already runs under that name, the brief asks me not to make structural changes — so confirm she's okay to rename.

6. **"Couples Mentorship" card — gender-neutral framing.** The research flags heteronormativity as a risk and Lee Harrington as a model for inclusive phrasing (§Lee Harrington). The current card says *"experienced couples guiding those newer to the dynamic"* which reads neutral to me, but flagging it for Casey's eye since she's better placed to judge her audience.

7. **Research gap: testimonials.** The research doesn't cover testimonial language and the site doesn't currently have any. Not proposing anything here — noting that if/when testimonials are added, their framing ("client," "member," "participant," "someone I worked with") should be decided deliberately since it'll lock in whether she reads as coach or community-host.
