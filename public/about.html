About

The story behind the practice, and the framework built from it.

Hi, I'm Casey!

For most of my life and career, the belief I ran on was my own. I had managers who didn't see my strengths, environments that asked me to hold space for everyone but myself, and institutions that told me to figure out how to work within broken systems. Nobody held the framework for me, so I learned to build one.

What this has taught me is that there are many people out there like me. Motivated, driven, passionate. But they're stuck. Stuck believing they can't be the main character of their own story.

This is where SPC was built from.

Through reading, podcasts, and hours and hours of research on leadership, parenting, and emotional intelligence, I created an engine that runs on two main philosophies: change is the only constant in life (so we might as well embrace it!), and you don't build strength by feeling powerful, you build it by showing up when you feel powerless.

I joke that I run my success out of spite. Then that joke became my drive. Building the storyline and redemption arc of my life motivated me to seek deeper growth, and through this I've found a passion for inspiring others to do the same.

This is what I did for over a decade without a title for it: standing with someone at the threshold between who they've been and who they're becoming, and giving them the structure, the honest feedback, and the belief they needed to get across.

The Threshold Framework

The framework SPC runs on has been tested and backed by the research and development of many different leaders in the space. One of the key pillars of my work is modeled after Jim Collins and Gino Wickman's "Right Person, Right Seat." This can apply to you, or your team. Do you have the right person? If the answer is yes, and they're still not performing, do you have them in the right seat? This is the foundation of our work.

Now think about yourself. Are you in the right seat? What seat would you occupy if there was nothing stopping you?

Below are a few of the key pillars that we will dive further into together in our sessions.

  • Hire for character, train for skill.
  • Right person, right seat, then build the seat.
  • Delegation is a curriculum.
  • Hold the framework, not the wheel.
  • Coaching should outlast authority.

Those are the principles. How each one plays out in your specific crossing is the work we do together inside an engagement, and it looks different for every person who crosses.

The Parallel Curriculum

This methodology has two laboratories. The first was a decade of leading and developing teams. The second was six years of motherhood, including the hardest kind, moving through the battles of addiction, divorce, and co-parenting.

The research done to show up as a good parent (stress response, developmental psychology, emotional coaching) turned out to be leadership research by another name. The same feedback loop that builds a confident kindergartner builds a confident manager. High expectations paired with high support is the quadrant developmental psychology calls authoritative parenting and leadership research calls transformational leadership. Same quadrant, both fields, one sentence:

I set the bar high because I know you'll clear it.
