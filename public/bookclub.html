The Growth Library

The books and thinkers behind the framework, curated for people mid-crossing.

The Threshold Framework wasn't built in a vacuum; it stands on decades of research about why capable people stall, and what actually gets them moving. This is that shelf, and if you're between who you've been and who you're becoming, it's a good place to start.

The Core Shelf

Transitions

William Bridges

The classic on the psychology of crossings: every transition is an ending, a neutral zone, and a beginning. It's threshold work, formalized forty years ago. If you read one book from this shelf, read this one.

Immunity to Change

Robert Kegan & Lisa Lahey

Harvard research on why capable people don't do what they fully intend to do: the hidden competing commitment. The academic backbone of the stall diagnosis used in every engagement.

Playing Big

Tara Mohr

The inner critic, visibility fear, and self-advocacy, especially for women stepping into bigger rooms. The book behind the self-advocate work in this practice.

The Coaching Habit

Michael Bungay Stanier

Seven questions that keep any developmental conversation honest. Useful whether you're being coached, coaching a team, or raising a self-advocate at home.

Good to Great

Jim Collins

Where "right people, right seats" comes from, named honestly as part of this framework's lineage. Collins tells you to find the right seat; the Threshold Framework is how you build one that doesn't exist yet.

The Prosperous Coach

Rich Litvin & Steve Chandler

For the builders: the case for creating anything meaningful one powerful conversation at a time, instead of through volume and noise. A model this practice runs on.

The Let Them Theory

Mel Robbins

Let other people think what they think and do what they do, then put all of that reclaimed energy into your own next move. Blunt, practical, and a perfect companion for anyone learning to be the main character of their own story.

Financial Feminist

Tori Dunlap

Money is leverage at every threshold, and Tori Dunlap of Her First $100K teaches women to negotiate, save, and invest without shame. A raise conversation backed by real numbers is a crossing this practice knows well.

Voices Worth Following

Jerry Colonna and the Reboot Podcast for depth and radical self-inquiry. Michael Bungay Stanier for the craft of better questions. Tara Mohr for women's leadership. Mel Robbins for the push to act on what you already know. Tori Dunlap of Her First $100K for financial confidence and negotiating your worth. Coaching for Leaders for the working manager. Follow one at a time; depth beats volume here too.

Read Along

Each month, the newsletter takes one idea from this shelf and turns it into something you can use that week: a question, an exercise, or a pattern to watch for in your own stall. There's no homework guilt and no book-report culture here, just one good idea at a time.
