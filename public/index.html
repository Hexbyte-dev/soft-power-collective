The Threshold

There's a specific place where most people stall: the gap between the idea and the operating reality. Between the role you've outgrown and the one that doesn't exist yet. Between knowing you're capable of more and acting on it.

That place you are stuck at is the threshold, and it's the work I was doing for over a decade before I had a name for it.

What I bring is the framework: the structure, the honest feedback, and the belief that you can. I've found through coaching many individuals from all different walks of life that often what holds them back is not the work itself, but the fear that this isn't their time. This isn't their story to be the main character in. My goal? To help you change that mindset and show you everything you are already capable of.

Documented Crossings

Years as a service tech → service manager

He had spent years as a service technician at another property before he came to us, capable and overlooked. His own supervisor had no interest in training a replacement, so I trained him myself, inside his current role, while managing that supervisor at the same time. He learned the next job before anyone offered it to him, and when the opening came he was already the obvious choice.

No experience → manager in six months

A leasing administrator stuck for years crossed to assistant manager in six months, then onto a management track at a national firm. The framework held; she supplied the motion.

20% under market → above-cap raise

A mid-tier agent was earning 20% under market and instead of being handed a raise, she was assigned the market research and presented her own bulletproof case. The company capped raises at 10%; she walked away with 22%.

Eight documented crossings, ten repeatable patterns, one framework.
